Classic Chocolate Chip

I personally believe cookies are the perfect dessert. They are just so versatile! They come in all shapes, sizes, and flavors. There is definitely a cookie for any occasion.
I love making my cookies with sourdough discard. It's a good way to use discard that has piled up, and it makes cookies somewhat gut healthy!

Ingredients

Tools

  • Stand mixer or hand mixer
  • Measuring spoons & measuring cups
  • Rubber spatula
  • Food scale
  • Baking sheet(s)
  • Parchment paper
  • 2 tbsp cookie scoop
  • Cooling rack

This recipe makes about 50 cookies
Feel free to half it to make about 25.

Instructions

  1. Using a stand mixer with the paddle attatchment or a hand mixer, mix together butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar. Beat at a medium speed and gradually increase until the mixture has a light and fluffy texture. This should take 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the sourdough discard, eggs, salt, and vanilla. Beat this together until fully combined.
  3. With either the mixer on low or with a rubber spatula by hand, slowly add in the flour, salt, baking soda, and baking powder
  4. Once the dry ingredients are almost fully incorporated, add in the chocolate chips. Do not overmix!
  5. (Optional) At this point, you can cover this and let it sit on your countertop for a few hours to allow the sourdough discard to ferment a bit more. You can also go straight into scooping and baking this dough with no wait!
  6. Preheat your oven to 350°F and line baking sheet with parchment paper
  7. Use your cookie scoop to create evenly shaped dough balls. Spread these out on your baking sheet(s) evenly.
  8. Place your cookies in the oven for 15 minutes or until the edges have formed and there is a slight golden color.
  9. Take your cookies out of the oven. Let them cool on the baking sheet for 2-5 minutes. Once the centers have solidified more, transfer them on top a cooling rack until they are cool enough to eat! This should take about 10-20 minutes.

Notes

  • Overmixing your cookie dough will result in a cakey texture. We don't want that! (Unless you like that)
